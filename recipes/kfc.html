Korean Fried Chicken

Home

Description

Korean fried chicken is an iconic must-have dish at drinking parties with friends. The difference between Korean fried chicken and regular, American fried chicken is the sweet, spicy, irresistable sauce that coats the fried shell. This recipe uses potato starch instead of regular flour to get that extra crispy exterior. After following these simple and easy steps, you'll have a delicious dish that'll bring the party to another level!

Ingredients

  • 3 pounds chicken legs
  • 2 cups potato starch
  • salt, pepper, and garlic powder to taste
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 bulb of garlic
  • 4 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon mustard
  • 3 tablespoons honey
  • 2 tablespoons brown sugar

Steps

  1. Get all ingredients out and prepared
  2. Season the chicken legs with salt, pepper, and garlic powder, mix, then let rest for a couple of minutes
  3. Get a pot of oil on medium-high heat
  4. Get potato starch in a bowl
  5. Once chicken is rested, coat with the potato starch and gently drop in the oil
  6. Fry the chicken for 8-10 minutes
  7. Take chicken out of oil and lay on a wired rack to let it cool down
  8. In another pot on medium heat, heat up olive oil
  9. Add in garlic and saut√© for a few minutes
  10. Add soy sauce, vinegar, mustard, and honey and stir until it slightly thickens
  11. Put heat to low and stir in brown sugar until fully dissolved and sauce has thickened even more
  12. Add in fried chicken and stir until each piece is coated completely
  13. Take out and enjoy with some beer!

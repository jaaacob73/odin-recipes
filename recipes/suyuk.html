Korean Suyuk

Home

Description

Suyuk, or bossam, is a traditional Korean pork dish starring flavorful, melt-in-your mouth boiled pork. The main flavor component of this recipe is a Korean paste called doenjang paste, which translates to fermented soybean paste. The paste gives the pork a deep, rich, salty flavor and it'll bring the dish closer to how it's traditionally made. This recipe might take some time to make, but following these simple steps will make the wait absolutely worth it!

Ingredients

  • 3 pounds pork belly
  • 4 green onions
  • 1 large white onion
  • handful of black peppercorns
  • 3/4 cup doenjang
  • 1 bulb of garlic

Steps

  1. Gather all the ingredients
  2. On a pan on high heat, sear all sides of pork belly until all sides have a nice brown color
  3. Peel and wash all the vegetables
  4. Chop up green onions into large, two-inch pieces
  5. In a pot of water (just enough water to cover the pork), add in all of the ingredients and bring to a boil
  6. Once it boils, bring it to a simmer and wait for one hour
  7. Take out the pork belly and cut it into nice thin pieces
  8. Enjoy with some steamed rice, kimchi, and vegetables
